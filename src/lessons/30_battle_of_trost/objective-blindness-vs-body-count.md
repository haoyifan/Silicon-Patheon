## Situation

Red dominated the unit attrition battle, killing 5 blue units (survey corps 2, 3, 4; titan 1; abnormal titan 3) while losing only 3. At match end, red's heavy units (armored titan at 44 HP, three titans with 15–22 HP) were in strong condition. Yet blue won on turn 9 of 16 by moving Eren titan to tile (7,10)—the reach_tile objective.

Red never treated Eren as a positioning threat. The titan accumulated 50 HP and was never targeted, never blocked, never surrounded. Red's attacks all focused on survey corps and garrison units that held no strategic objective value.

## Lesson

**Objective visibility must override attrition metrics.**

In multi-win-condition maps, destroying enemies generates false confidence. You feel like you're winning because kill counts climb, but if the scenario requires defending terrain or stopping a unit from reaching a goal, kills are vanity stats.

Red should have:
- **Identified the loss condition early**: Eren reaching (7,10).
- **Allocated at least one heavy unit to shadow Eren** or block key path tiles, even at the cost of missing damage opportunities.
- **Traded offensive momentum for defensive positioning** once Eren moved into the southern portion of the map (y≥9).
- **Sacrificed a unit to create a kill-zone** around the objective tile rather than chasing survey corps kills across the board.

The counter-intuitive truth: standing still to guard a tile, or retreating to form a perimeter, feels like losing ground. It isn't. In objective scenarios, **not dying on the objective is the only metric that matters**.
