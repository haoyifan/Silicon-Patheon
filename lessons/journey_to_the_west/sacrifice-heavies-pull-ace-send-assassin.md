## Situation

As red on *Journey to the West*, I fielded a roster of demons against blue's pilgrim party. Blue's composition had a clear lynchpin: **Sun Wukong** (42 HP, 14 ATK, move 5) — a wrecking ball that could solo any demon in two hits. Blue's win condition was to escort **Tang Monk** (the VIP) safely across the map; mine was to kill Tang Monk before turn 20.

The problem: Tang Monk hid behind a wall of disciples, and Sun Wukong roamed freely as an interceptor. Any direct rush at the VIP would be shredded by Wukong before reaching the backline.

## What Happened

On turn 1 I moved Bull Demon and Demon King to the **far flank** (x=6–7), away from Tang Monk's travel corridor along x=0. This looked passive — neither unit threatened the VIP — but it created a problem blue couldn't ignore. Wukong moved to x=7 and killed Bull Demon on turn 2, then pursued Demon King through turns 4–6, spending three full turns chasing and finishing those heavies on the wrong side of the board.

Meanwhile, **Spider Spirit 2** (SPD 8, move 5) quietly advanced along the y-axis toward Tang Monk. Blue's remaining escorts — Pigsy, Sandy, Bai Long Ma — engaged my other disposable units (Spider Spirit 1 and Skeleton 3) in the midfield. By turn 5, Spider Spirit 2 landed 10 damage on Tang Monk. By turn 7, Tang Monk had drifted to (0,1) with only 6 HP remaining and no adjacent bodyguard. Spider Spirit 2 closed from (0,2) and delivered the kill.

Red lost four of five units. Blue lost zero combat units. Red still won.

## Lesson

**When the win condition is assassination, not attrition, trade material deliberately to displace the enemy's strongest defender.**

The generalizable principle:

1. **Identify the enemy's ace** — the unit that single-handedly guards the objective.
2. **Offer bait worth chasing** — heavies that the ace *can* kill but that cost it positional turns.
3. **Stage a fast, cheap finisher** on the opposite vector while the ace is committed.

You don't need to out-fight the enemy army. You need to make their best piece solve the wrong problem for long enough that a throwaway unit solves yours. A 7-HP spider spirit wins the game just as hard as a full-health demon king — victory doesn't care who's left standing.
