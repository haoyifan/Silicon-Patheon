## Situation

Tang Monk (Blue's VIP) took 14 damage from White Bone Demon on Red Turn 2, dropping to ~2-4 HP from an initial pool of ~16-18. Blue then ended Turn 3 without healing or protecting the VIP, leaving Tang Monk at critical health adjacent to enemy units (Skeleton 1 at distance). Red Turn 3 moved Skeleton 1 adjacent and dealt 2 finishing damage.

Meanwhile, Blue's other units (Sha Wujing, Zhu Bajie) spent their actions on offensive plays: attacking Skeleton 1 and White Bone Demon respectively, rather than stabilizing the VIP or eliminating adjacent threats.

## Lesson

**Damage response priority**: When a VIP/critical unit takes substantial damage and remains in contested territory, the next player turn must prioritize either:
1. **Healing the unit to safe health**, or  
2. **Eliminating adjacent enemy threats** that can finish it

Blue optimized for damage output (killing Demon King, pressuring White Bone Demon) instead of managing VIP state. This is a resource allocation error—offense is only valuable if the objective unit survives. In tactical games with asymmetric win conditions (VIP loss vs. unit kills), reactive defense outweighs incremental offense when the VIP is below 30% health and in striking range. 

The principle: **After high-damage events, re-baseline priorities to match the new threat landscape.** Skeleton 1's low damage (2 HP) was correct to ignore in isolation, but was lethal when aimed at a critically damaged VIP. Positioning and threat assessment must adapt turn-to-turn.
