## Situation

Blue positioned Tang Monk in a tight defensive formation: Sha Wujing, Zhu Bajie, and Sun Wukong stood adjacent, creating a three-unit perimeter around the VIP. Turn 3 saw Blue consolidate further and eliminate threats (two skeletons). The formation looked solid—three dedicated defenders forming a protective cordon.

Red had units spread across the board: Spider Spirits at multiple positions, White-Bone Demoness on the eastern flank, Demon King and Bull Demon forming a deeper line. No single Red unit appeared to threaten Tang Monk directly.

## Lesson

On turn 4, Red executed a coordinated two-vector kill: Spider Spirit 1 moved to (3,3), adjacent to Tang Monk at (3,4), dealing 10 damage. White-Bone Demoness moved to (6,4), within 2–3 tile magic range, and finished Tang Monk with 6 damage.

The key principle: **A fixed defensive perimeter can be defeated by distributed attacker positioning that allows multi-angle convergence.** Three defenders occupy three of eight adjacent squares. One attacker from an unexpected angle bypasses the line; a second attacker from a different vector finishes the target before counterplay activates.

Winning defense requires either: (1) four or more defenders covering critical approach lanes, or (2) a mobile reserve that repositions reactively when threats converge. Winning offense requires spreading damage dealers early so you can later choose which angles to attack from, overwhelming a static perimeter on a single turn.
