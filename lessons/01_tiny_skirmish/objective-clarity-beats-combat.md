## Situation
Blue faced a 2v2 engagement with a seize objective at (5,5). Rather than pursuing optimal trades, Blue executed a coordinated push toward the objective. The archer advanced from (5,0) → (5,4) → (5,5) over three turns, with the knight consolidating at (5,2). When the archer reached the seize point on turn 5, Blue won—despite taking 5 damage (13 hp remaining) and without destroying enemy units.

Red's units remained scattered: the archer never left the (0,y) column; the knight engaged reactively at (4,4) without contesting the objective.

## Lesson
**In objective-based scenarios, the path to win beats the path to optimal trades.** Blue won not by force preservation or favorable exchanges, but by:

1. **Early recognition**: Turn-1 positioning (both units toward x=5) signaled immediate intent toward the objective.
2. **Disciplined advance**: The archer maintained a direct line despite taking damage en route; movement was purposeful, not defensive.
3. **Concentration**: Both units converged on the objective zone, creating implied pressure even without direct support.

Red's scattered positioning—especially an archer remaining far from action—suggests a reactive, engagement-focused strategy that ceded the initiative.

**Generalization**: Understand your win condition in turn 1. Commit all available resources toward it first; use remaining capacity for defense. In seize/hold/escort objectives, clarity of purpose and coordinated focus often beats positional flexibility or superior individual trades. You win by achieving the objective, not by optimizing the path there.
