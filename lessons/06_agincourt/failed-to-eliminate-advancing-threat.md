## Situation

French knight_7 entered blue's defensive zone critically wounded at 6 HP after absorbing 15 damage from three separate longbowman attacks. Rather than commit focused fire to finish the unit, blue's remaining units chose to wait while the knight advanced unopposed into the goal zone at (2,13).

Blue controlled the tactical area—multiple longbowmen were in range, Henry V and man-at-arms units held the centerline—but failed to converge damage on a single high-threat target at a critical moment.

## Lesson

In reach-goal scenarios, **attrition of mobile threats outranks area control**. A single surviving unit can penetrate defensive positions if left alive. Blue's error was treating the knight as a delayed problem rather than an immediate one.

Key principle: When an opponent's unit enters your defensive perimeter and threatens the objective, killing it becomes the only move. Partial damage followed by passive defense assumes the threat will be contained—but in an asymmetric game where one unit reaching a line wins, survival is victory.

Blue's positioning was sound. The execution was sound. The *decision* to spread damage across turns instead of concentrating fire for the kill lost the match in turn 3-4 of the engagement. The lesson transfers: **against mobile goal-strikers, trade any other objective for threat elimination.**
