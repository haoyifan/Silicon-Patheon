## Situation

Red faced overwhelming numerical disadvantage: ~10 units (mostly weak White Zetsu minions) versus Blue's ~20 units (8 named shinobi + 8 allied shinobi). Traditional attrition warfare was unsustainable. The scenario's win condition was eliminating the blue VIP (Naruto).

## Lesson

Red identified that Madara was the sole potent combat unit and laser-focused on a single, achievable objective: Naruto elimination. Over three consecutive turns, Madara relentlessly hunted Naruto across the map, dealing 9–9–7 cumulative damage. Blue's larger army became inert—without a coordinated offense, units waited passively while their VIP was systematically pursued.

Red's White Zetsu sacrifices were tactical staging; they cleared sightlines and delayed Blue's response, but were secondary to the primary objective. By turn 6, Naruto was dead and the match concluded—7 turns out of a possible 16.

**Key insight:** When outnumbered, abandon attempts to win in your opponent's domain (attrition, positional control). Instead, identify one non-negotiable victory condition and concentrate resources toward it. A focused, unambiguous objective beats distributed mediocrity. Blue's numerical edge was neutralized because it had no answer to single-target hunter strategy.
