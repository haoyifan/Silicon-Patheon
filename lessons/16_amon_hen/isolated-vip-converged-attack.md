## Situation

Blue advanced Frodo to (10, 5) on turn 1 without establishing a defensive perimeter. No blue units were positioned to intercept or shield the VIP. Red recognized this gap and moved Uruk-hai 13 and 12 to converge on the exposed position. A two-unit attack—7 damage plus 1 finishing blow—eliminated Frodo before blue could mount a defense.

## Lesson

In VIP scenarios, **isolation beyond the defensive line = instant vulnerability**. When a protected unit outpaces its support, attackers can exploit the gap through concentration of force. Even moderate damage from two coordinated sources (14 total) bypasses whatever survivability the VIP had.

The broader principle: **defensive depth matters more than aggressive positioning early**. Blue sacrificed protection for map control, but a VIP kill in turn 2 erases any positional advantage. In contrast, keeping the VIP clustered with supporting units (healers, tanks, damage dealers) forces attackers to either:
1. Break through the cluster (slow, costly)
2. Spend multiple turns softening defenses (gives blue time to counterattack)

Red's win wasn't about raw stats—it was about punishing a structural error in blue's defense planning. The lesson transfers: always map your VIP's defensive radius *before* moving forward. If no allied units can reach the VIP within one enemy turn, don't send it there yet.
